About Me

I'm right now a postgraduate student studying theoretical physics under the supervision of Prof. Yu Shi, in Fudan University, Shanghai.

My present research is mainly about pseudo quantum simulation based on GPU and its applications.

Normally, for a system with qubits less than 30, the advantage of pseudo quantum computing is prominent as all the information of the quantum system we simulate are available at any time without the problem of decoherence. Pseudo quantum computing enables us not only to verify and develop algorithm, but to solve some quantum problems, especially in the field of many-body system and lattice gauge theory, and this is what my recent research focus on.

I'm also an amateur in sociology and philosophy.

Research

One recent work I have accomplished is the pseudo digital quantum simulation of gauge-Higgs model, whose ground state is considered to have two distinct phases (deconfinement and Higgs-confinement). Previous studies about this model are mainly about its classical version and by MC, which have difficulty to determine the properties near the 'tricritical' point.

By pseudo digital quantum simulation, we can directly investigate the quantum model and preserve the real-time dynamic along the evolution. Although the lattice is small, we can still achieve similar results outside the tricritical point, and give an evidence of the picture near the tricritical point.
